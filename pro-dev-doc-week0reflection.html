Weekly Reflection

Week 0:


I enjoyed the week getting to learn about my peers. Personalities are starting to show like turtles poking their heads out of their shell. I had a lot of fun working in the group setting. I feel like I am starting to learn how my role will develop as time progresses throughout the duration of the bootcamp.


The snap challenges are definitely a good measure of skill level for the class. I feel like I'm able to apply the learnings from lecture to completing the challenges. I may have missed a few questions, but I read documentation or found someone to help explain an answer. The week's teachings/lectures were on the topics of architecture & xAMP stack, the Deep Dive Coding style guide, and data design. I wasn't confident in my deployment skills for a bit. I was struggling with that for the first few days of instruction. I'm becoming more confident in some skills, but I also see where I need to improve. The data design content is familiar from a time when I took mySQL courses at CNM. I felt good working on that project. It was a good test of my knowledge and I'm excited to continue through these transitions.


I attended two events. On Tuesday, April 10th, I attended a Meetup hosted by Deep Dive Coding including a collaboration with a representative from Julia and a coworking facilty by the name of FreeRange. Jane from Julia talked the Julia programming language and it's features. A few tech guys from the local ABQ area attended as well as a few of my peers from the cohort. The second event on Thursday, April 12th, was hosted by Bernalillo County and the new Lobo Rainforest facility. Entitled "Colliding the Future", the concept was to figure how to bridge the connection between younger and older generations in the workforce. I met a few individuals who had some good words.


This weekend, I plan to continue to work on homework assignments and more code. I also hope to order some new headphones, a USB-C hub, and possibly a new mouse.
